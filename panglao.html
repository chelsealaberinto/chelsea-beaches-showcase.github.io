Panglao Island

Sorsogon, Philippines

About

Panglao Island in Bohol, Philippines, is one of the country’s premier tropical destinations, famed for its white-sand beaches, crystal-clear waters, and vibrant marine life. Often compared to Boracay for its beauty but with a more relaxed atmosphere, Panglao offers the perfect blend of luxury and tranquility. It’s a gateway to world-class diving spots, natural attractions, and Bohol’s warm island culture.

Location

Located southwest of Bohol Island and connected to Tagbilaran City by two bridges, Panglao Island is easily accessible for both local and international travelers. The island is surrounded by the Bohol Sea, boasting beaches like Alona, Dumaluan, and Danao, all known for their fine white sand and turquoise waters. Beneath the surface, Panglao’s waters are home to rich coral gardens and diverse marine species, making it a paradise for divers and snorkelers.

Nature and Environment

The island is part of the Bohol Marine Triangle, a protected area known for its exceptional marine biodiversity. Its coral reefs are among the healthiest in the Philippines, sheltering sea turtles, colorful fish, and rare aquatic plants. Inland, visitors can explore Hinagdanan Cave — a natural limestone cave with an underground lagoon — or visit nearby eco-tourism sites promoting conservation and sustainable tourism.

Ambiance and Experience

Panglao blends laid-back island charm with modern comfort. By day, visitors can swim, kayak, or dive in its clear waters, and by night, unwind at beachside restaurants with live music under the stars. The island’s easygoing atmosphere and friendly locals make it a perfect getaway for couples, families, and solo travelers who want both relaxation and a touch of adventure.

Accommodations and Tourism

Panglao features a variety of places to stay, from luxurious beachfront resorts and cozy boutique hotels to affordable hostels and homestays. Most accommodations are near Alona Beach, the lively center of the island’s tourism scene. Visitors can savor fresh seafood, join island-hopping tours to Balicasag and Virgin Islands, or watch dolphins at sunrise. With its breathtaking scenery, vibrant marine life, and warm community, Panglao Island remains one of the Philippines’ favorite destinations — a perfect mix of tropical paradise and mindful tourism.
