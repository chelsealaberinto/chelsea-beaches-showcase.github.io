Islas de Gigantes, Iloilo

Iloilo, Philippines

About

Islas de Gigantes, located off the coast of Carles in Iloilo, is a stunning group of islands known for its dramatic limestone cliffs, white sand beaches, and crystal-clear waters. Often called the “Hidden Paradise of Iloilo,” it offers a perfect escape for those seeking natural beauty, adventure, and serenity away from the city crowds. With its combination of pristine beaches, mysterious caves, and fresh seafood, Islas de Gigantes is a must-visit destination for eco-tourists and nature lovers.

Location

Islas de Gigantes lies on the northeastern part of Iloilo Province, in the municipality of Carles. It consists of two main islands — Gigantes Norte and Gigantes Sur — surrounded by smaller islets. The area is blessed with towering rock formations, hidden lagoons, and powdery white sand beaches. The calm, turquoise waters and gentle sea breeze make it an ideal spot for relaxation and exploration.

Nature and Environment

Islas de Gigantes is part of a protected marine area known for its rich biodiversity. The surrounding waters are home to coral reefs and abundant marine life, making it an excellent site for snorkeling and diving. The islands’ limestone formations and hidden caves showcase nature’s artistry, while local communities practice sustainable fishing to preserve the ecosystem.

Ambiance and Experience

The islands offer a peaceful and authentic atmosphere, far from the hustle of commercial resorts. Visitors can enjoy island hopping, cave exploration, and swimming in secluded beaches. The view of the sunset from the cliffs or shoreline is breathtaking, painting the sea in shades of gold and violet. Nights are serene, with only the sound of waves and the glimmer of stars above.

Accommodations and Tourism

Visitors can choose from cozy beachfront resorts, simple homestays, or eco-friendly cottages that blend with the island’s natural charm. Many locals offer fresh seafood meals, with scallops being the island’s specialty. Tourism here emphasizes sustainability and community livelihood, allowing guests to enjoy the beauty of the islands while supporting local fishermen and families.
