Manjuyod White Sandbar

Negros Oriental, Philippines

About

Manjuyod White Sandbar in Negros Oriental, Philippines, is often called the “Maldives of the Philippines” for its stunning stretch of white sand that appears during low tide. This natural sandbar, surrounded by crystal-clear turquoise waters, offers a serene escape perfect for swimming, snorkeling, and dolphin watching. Its pristine beauty and simplicity make it one of the top eco-tourism spots in Central Visayas.

Location

Situated off the coast of Bais City in Negros Oriental, Manjuyod White Sandbar stretches approximately 7 kilometers across the Tanon Strait, the narrow body of water separating Negros Island from Cebu. It is easily accessible via a 15-minute boat ride from the Bais City wharf. When the tide is low, the sandbar emerges from the sea, creating a stunning expanse of white sand surrounded by shimmering blue waters. The backdrop of the distant mountains and the horizon makes it a truly postcard-perfect destination.

Nature and Environment

The sandbar is surrounded by rich marine life, including colorful fish, seagrass beds, and corals. The nearby Tanon Strait is a protected area known for its population of dolphins and whales. Local authorities and communities work together to maintain its cleanliness and promote responsible tourism so that visitors can continue to enjoy its natural charm for years to come.

Ambiance and Experience

Manjuyod is the kind of place where you can slow down and just enjoy the moment. You can take a walk along the sandbar, swim in calm waters, or rent one of the floating cottages for a relaxing day by the sea. Many visitors also take early morning boat tours to watch dolphins play in the Tanon Strait — one of the most memorable experiences in Negros Oriental. The calm, open setting and gentle sea breeze make it a perfect spot to unwind and connect with nature.

Accommodations and Tourism

While there are no hotels on the sandbar itself, nearby Bais City and Dumaguete offer comfortable accommodations — from simple guesthouses to beach resorts. Boat operators in Bais organize day tours that include dolphin watching, seafood lunches, and island-hopping. The experience is simple but authentic, reflecting the warmth and hospitality of the locals. In short, Manjuyod White Sandbar is a peaceful paradise — a place where the sea, sky, and sand come together beautifully, offering a glimpse of the Philippines’ natural wonders at their best.
