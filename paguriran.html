Paguriran Island

Sorsogon, Philippines

About

Paguriran Island in Bacon District, Sorsogon, is a hidden tropical gem known for its crystal-clear turquoise lagoon encircled by dramatic limestone formations. Though called an island, it’s actually a rock formation connected to the mainland by a shallow sandbar, making it accessible by foot during low tide. The island’s natural lagoon, sometimes compared to a mini version of Palawan’s lagoons, offers a serene and photogenic setting perfect for swimming, snorkeling, and relaxing in nature.

Location

Found in Barangay Sawanga, Bacon, Sorsogon, Paguriran Island lies about 15 kilometers from Sorsogon City. The area features a picturesque blend of white sand, coral rocks, and calm blue-green waters that contrast beautifully with the rugged cliffs surrounding the lagoon. The shallow shoreline makes it family-friendly and ideal for wading or snorkeling. The island faces the vast Sorsogon Bay, providing a panoramic view of the horizon and nearby coastal villages.

Nature and Environment

The lagoon’s clear waters are home to small marine species, coral formations, and sea grasses, reflecting the area’s ecological richness. The surrounding vegetation provides shade and enhances the natural beauty of the landscape. Local communities actively maintain the area’s cleanliness, promoting eco-friendly tourism and sustainable visitor practices.

Ambiance and Experience

Paguriran has a calm, rustic charm that feels worlds away from the busy crowds of popular tourist spots. Friendly locals welcome visitors, who can spend the day swimming, cliff diving, or wandering around the rock pools when the tide is low. Its peaceful atmosphere makes it a perfect hideaway for nature lovers and photographers looking for a quiet escape.

Accommodations and Tourism

While Paguriran Island itself has no hotels or large resorts, several beachfront cottages, nipa huts, and homestays in nearby Bacon and Sorsogon City offer comfortable lodging for visitors. Tourists can rent huts for day use or stay overnight in budget-friendly accommodations with ocean views. The local tourism office and community manage visitor activities, offering guided tours, boat rides, and fresh seafood dining experiences. Paguriran Island stands as one of Sorsogon’s most underrated yet enchanting destinations — a perfect spot for travelers seeking simplicity, natural beauty, and an authentic local experience.
